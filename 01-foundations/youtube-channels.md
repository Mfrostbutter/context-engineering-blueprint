# YouTube Channels for Learning AI

Curated list of channels worth subscribing to. Organized by level and focus.

---

## Start here — conceptual foundations

**3Blue1Brown**
`youtube.com/@3blue1brown`
Visual explanations of math and neural networks. No coding required. The video series "Neural Networks" is the best visual explanation of how AI actually works.
- Best video: "But what is a neural network?" (watch the whole series)
- Also see: "Attention in transformers, visually explained"

**AI Explained**
`youtube.com/@aiexplained-official`
Clear, accessible breakdowns of AI papers and concepts. Good for understanding what new models actually do.
- Best starting point: His explainers on GPT-4, Claude, and Gemini releases

---

## News and tools — staying current

**Matt Wolfe**
`youtube.com/@mreflow`
The best channel for tracking new AI tools as they launch. Weekly round-ups of what dropped, what's worth trying, and what's hype.
- Best use: Watch weekly to stay current without reading every tech blog

**Fireship**
`youtube.com/@Fireship`
Fast, high-quality explainers on coding and AI tools. His "100 seconds" series is excellent for quick mental models.
- Best videos: "Claude Code in 100 seconds", "Git Explained in 100 Seconds", "Cursor AI just changed everything"

**Two Minute Papers**
`youtube.com/@TwoMinutePapers`
Short summaries of new AI research papers. Good for keeping up with what's being published without reading the papers.

---

## Technical depth — for when you want to go deeper

**Andrej Karpathy**
`youtube.com/@AndrejKarpathy`
Former Tesla AI director and OpenAI founding member. His videos are long but worth it — he explains how LLMs work from first principles, often by building one from scratch.
- Best video: "Let's build GPT: from scratch, in code, spelled out"
- Also see: "Intro to Large Language Models" (1 hour overview, excellent)

**Yannic Kilcher**
`youtube.com/@YannicKilcher`
Deep dives on AI research papers. More technical than AI Explained but very thorough.
- Best for: Understanding landmark papers like "Attention Is All You Need"

**Sam Witteveen**
`youtube.com/@samwitteveenai`
Practical LLM development — building with APIs, agents, and tools. More hands-on than conceptual.
- Best for: When you're ready to start building with Claude's API

---

## Automation and workflows

**n8n (official)**
`youtube.com/@n8n-io`
Official n8n channel. Workflow tutorials, AI node integrations, and use case walkthroughs.
- Start with their "Getting Started" playlist

**Automate All The Things**
`youtube.com/@automateallthethings`
Real-world n8n workflows built and explained. Great for seeing what's actually possible.

---

## Recommended watch order for beginners

1. 3Blue1Brown — "But what is a neural network?" (4-part series, ~1 hour total)
2. Andrej Karpathy — "Intro to Large Language Models" (1 hour)
3. AI Explained — any recent model explainer
4. Fireship — "Claude Code in 100 Seconds" or "Cursor AI just changed everything"
5. Matt Wolfe — subscribe and watch weekly

After that, pick based on your direction:
- Building automations → n8n official channel
- Writing code with AI → Sam Witteveen
- Tracking research → Two Minute Papers or Yannic Kilcher
